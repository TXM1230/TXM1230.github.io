Xinming Tu

I am an undergraduate student at Peking University, Biology & Computer Science Double major.
My research interest is using computational methods to understand the algorithm and principles of life, especially genomics.
Recently, I focus on decoding gene regulation underlying cell fate decisions using high-throughput single cell data.
Now, I am a member of Gao lab. And last year, I did summer research in Hongyu's lab at Yale.
Besides, I spent six months on doing research at Machine Learning Group of Microsoft Research Lab – Asia as a research intern. I also worked with Dr.Sheng Wang remotely on interpretable machine learning methods on single cell last summer.

Project

Using the statistical view to understand and improve deep learning model in bio-sequence data[Code]

  • Drive a statistical view to understand the powerful learning ability of a convolutional neural network (CNN) in bio-sequence data
  • Develop a exact kernel-to-PWM transformation and the theory : the log-likelihood of the generating sequence is equivalence the convolution value of the kernel
  • Propose an expectation pooling which is inspired from the EM algorithm and can be interpreted from the view of statistical models

Proposing a novel convolution-based model-vCNN (Variable CNN) for effective bio-motif detection via the adaptive kernel length [Code]

  • Develop a theoretical framework for quantitatively modeling the relationship of convolution kernel shape and the motif detection effectiveness
  • Design and propose a novel convolution layer, vCNN (Variable CNN), for effective bio-motif detection via the adaptive kernel length at runtime

A New Metric for the Single cell Gene Expression Based on Interaction Network

  • Use Markov Random Field with the biology prior to estimate the distribution of Expression
  • Design a new Metric of cell-cell distance based on the probability distribution
